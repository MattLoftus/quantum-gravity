1Track 1: Universal Predictions Survey

A systematic literature review of 80+ papers (2024–2026) identified 10 convergent predictions across independent approaches to quantum gravity. The convergence is striking: approaches developed from completely different starting assumptions (discrete vs. continuous, canonical vs. covariant, perturbative vs. non-perturbative) arrive at the same physical predictions.

The 10 Convergent Predictions

1. Spectral Dimension Flow: ds = 4 → ~2 Strongest

▼

The spectral dimension of spacetime flows from ~4 at macroscopic scales to ~2 at the Planck scale. This is the single most reproduced prediction across all approaches.

Approaches in agreement (8+): CDT, Asymptotic Safety, Horava-Lifshitz, LQG/Spin Foams, Causal Sets, Noncommutative Geometry, String Theory (Hagedorn limit), Multifractional spacetimes.

Physical mechanism (Carlip 2017): In d=2, Newton's constant G is dimensionless, making gravity renormalizable. Possibly connected to BKL-type "asymptotic silence" near the Planck scale.

Our finding (Exp04): The ds ≈ 2 crossing is a generic graph property, not specific to causal structure. The physically meaningful signal is a plateau at ds = 2 and the specific flow profile, not the mere fact of crossing ds = 2.

2. Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy: S = A/4

▼

The universal benchmark for black hole entropy, reproduced by all serious approaches to quantum gravity (some requiring parameter tuning, e.g., the Barbero-Immirzi parameter in LQG).

3. Logarithmic Corrections: S = A/4 + Clog ln(A) + ... Key Discriminator

▼

This is where approaches start disagreeing — making it the most promising target for distinguishing between theories.

  • LQG: Clog = −3/2 (topological, field-independent)
  • String theory / Euclidean gravity: Clog depends on matter content

This is the sharpest point of disagreement and the most promising target for theoretical computation or eventual observation.

2Track 2: Causal Set Simulations

Causal set theory posits that spacetime is fundamentally a discrete partial order, with the causal relations between spacetime events as the primary structure. We implemented and tested Classical Sequential Growth (CSG) dynamics, Benincasa-Dowker action MCMC on 2-orders, CDT simulations, dimension estimators, and spectral analysis across 20+ experiments. The culminating result is the discovery of interval entropy as a novel order parameter for the BD phase transition.

Experiment 02: CSG Emergent Dimension Too 1D

▼

Transitive percolation (the simplest CSG model) produces causets that are too 1-dimensional. Even at low coupling (p=0.05), global MM dimension ≈ 1.75. Scale-dependent measurement shows mild flow (d=1.88 at UV → 1.64 at IR for p=0.10).

Conclusion: Simple transitive percolation (tk = tk) is known to converge to "semi-orders" (Brightwell & Luczak 2015) and cannot produce 4D manifold-like causets. Need the general CSG coupling constants {tn} or entirely new dynamics.

Experiment 13: Systematic CSG Coupling Scan Major Negative

▼

Scanned 274 coupling configurations across 6 parametric families (exponential, exp-poly, bell, step, mixed, power-law). Also ran Nelder-Mead and differential evolution optimization.

Result: NO coupling constants found that produce manifold-like causets at ANY dimension. All candidates either: (a) produce near-total-orders (d≈1), or (b) produce sparse antichains (high MM dim but ds≈1). The step kmax=1 remains the closest to 4D by MM dimension, but spectral dimension confirms it is tree-like.

Interpretation: CSG dynamics alone is insufficient to generate spacetime. The sequential “one element at a time” growth creates inherently tree/chain-like structures. Extensions needed: quantum CSG (path integral), non-local dynamics, or Benincasa-Dowker action.
